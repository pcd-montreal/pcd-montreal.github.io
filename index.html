February 9, 2019 - 10h ~ 18h



Processing Community Day Montréal 2019 is an inclusive event to celebrate art, code, and diversity. It features presentations from various people who write code opening to its creative potential, workshops related to creative coding, open source software and live coding. PCD Montréal 2019 invites you to share diverse views and discoveries related with FLOSS, art, philosophy, pedagogy, etc.

This event is part of a large network of similar events that will happen around the world (in Bangalore, Los Angeles, Mexico, São Paulo, Stockholm, Tokyo, etc.), brought together by the Processing Foundation, a non-profit organization promoting software literacy within the visual arts, and visual literacy within technology-related fields — and dedicated to making these fields accessible to diverse communities.

  // PROGRAM  


NEO//QAB - Rilla Khaled

NEO//QAB (performance) is a speculative garment that provides a darkly provocative take on religious tolerance, empowering individuals to deal with those who resist cultural assimilation by replacing them visually. Built with Processing, Kinect, Twine, and JavaScript, NEO//QAB is a two-person experience, involving a wearer of a full-body garment, and a controller, who controls the appearance of the garment through a mobile device. NEO//QAB foregrounds what is uncomfortable about enforcing rules for how others can dress, and opens a conversation around religion, immigration, legislation, and clothes.

Creative Coding: Beyond The Loops - Kofi Oduro

This talk explores the different settings and ways that Creative Coding can be used beyond the typical audiovisual lens, it is often associated with. Even though it is a digital medium, it can be utilized as a great tool for non digital entities. The talk will also touch upon potential benefits that can arise from engaging in a creative mindset when coding.

yi' (callejón de luz) - Rodrigo Velasco + Patrick Gauvin

yi’ (talk + performance) is a making-thinking process that involves an evolving audiovisual exploration through a constant conversation between live coding and photogrammetry. In this spectrum, sound and image are fields of relation. yi' moves ideas from the real-analog to the algorithmic realm with an interest in not to recreate, but to 'dérive', finding new openings and giving shape to unexpected encounters through different rhythms, sonorities and resonances in movement.
